Tutorial

Note

This version of Trinity Capital is a Pilot intended for showcase and developement

Due to this, the features of the application are minimal and only designed to show off the functionality, and provide students and staff with an idea of how the application could work

If you have any ideas for things such as lesson content, functionality, or any ways that you, the student, feel would make this application a better learning tool, please use the feedback form at the end of the final lesson

Bugs should be reported using the Bugs and feedback link. That link is designed for issues with the application.

Thank you so much for piloting this app and happy banking!

Note

In the full version of the Application, students will be able to open an unlimited number of varying different types of accounts including accounts such as CDs (Certificate of Deposit)

Opening these accounts will be ran through the bank splash page once its developed

Note

In this pilot, the Routing number is not available to view. However, in later versions, there will be functionality added to allow students to view their critical account information such as their account number, routing number, and other key info.

Note

For Staff

Loans functionality will be greatly updated in later versions. There will be things added such as a simulated credit check, loan approval, approval delay, and a payback time. For now, loans will just push the transaction.

Note

For Students

Loans in the real world are very serious and should not be taken lightly. In this pilot, loans are easy to request and have almost no consequences, however, this will change in the final version. In the real world, there are major consequences for not paying back loans, and they are often hard to manage with your other bills. BE CAREFUL.

Note

Congrats on making it through the Tutorial! You can now explore the application further, or move straight into the next lesson

Please do not commit any transactions at this time. Simply look at each page, and use the back button to return to the main page

Commiting any transactions prematurely can cause problems for the next lessons
