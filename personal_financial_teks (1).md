# Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS)
## Personal Financial Literacy and Economics (One-Half Credit)
### 19 Texas Administrative Code ยง113.76

**Implementation:** 2022-2023 school year onward.

**Course Credit:** One-half credit; recommended for grades 11-12. Students may not receive credit for both this course and the standalone Personal Financial Literacy course.

---

### (c) Introduction
1. **Economic Way of Thinking:** Emphasizes using economic reasoning for personal financial decisions. Students learn to anticipate and address financial challenges across their lifetime, integrating both economic and personal financial planning concepts.
2. **Integration of Economics and Finance:** Builds on K-12 social studies and math TEKS. Covers microeconomics (supply, demand, industry structures, government policy) and macroeconomics (economic systems, goals, and problems like inflation and unemployment). Extends personal finance topics such as budgeting, saving, investing, and protecting income. Students examine how individual financial choices impact the broader economy.
3. **Course Framework:** Created under TEC ยง28.025(b-22). Two-thirds of instructional time must focus on personal financial literacy and one-third on economics.
4. **Celebrate Freedom Week:** Instruction must include U.S. founding documents (Declaration of Independence, Constitution, Bill of Rights) and their relevance to American history and civic life.
5. **Civic Understanding:** Students identify how government actions meet or fail to meet the ideals in founding documents.
6. **Government Structure:** Understand the principles of a constitutional republic.
7. **Clarifying Terms:** "Including" means required content; "such as" provides examples.

### (d) Knowledge and Skills
#### (1) Economics Fundamentals
Students understand the fundamental concepts of economics.
- (A) Analyze scarcity, choice, and opportunity costs in decision making.
- (B) Interpret production-possibilities curves.
- (C) Apply cost-benefit analysis.
- (D) Use the circular flow model to show household, firm, and government interactions.
- (E) Evaluate how supply and demand determine prices and quantities.
- (F) Interpret supply and demand graphs (equilibrium, surpluses, shortages).
- (G) Analyze how non-price determinants affect equilibrium.
- (H) Explain how supply and demand exist in resource and product markets.

#### (2) Macroeconomic Policies and Personal Finance
- (A) Identify and explain types of taxes (progressive, regressive) and their roles.
- (B) Examine reasons for federal and payroll taxes (Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid).
- (C) Explain how all systems exist on a spectrum from market to command.
- (D) Describe benefits of the U.S. free enterprise system.
- (E) Discuss macroeconomic goals: full employment, price stability, growth.
- (F) Explain fiscal policy effects on inflation, interest rates, unemployment.
- (G) Explain monetary policy effects by the Federal Reserve on the same factors.

#### (3) Investing in Education and Skills
- (A) Analyze education and training's effect on lifetime earnings.
- (B) Investigate postsecondary education options, costs, and funding.
- (C) Compare trade schools, certifications, community college, university paths.
- (D) Examine student loan options, scholarships, and long-term debt management.

#### (4) Earning and Income
- (A) Identify sources of income: wages, salaries, interest, dividends, entrepreneurship.
- (B) Explain gross vs. net income and payroll deductions.
- (C) Analyze career choices based on income potential, education, and personal goals.

#### (5) Budgeting and Financial Planning
- (A) Create and evaluate personal budgets.
- (B) Identify fixed and variable expenses.
- (C) Evaluate short- and long-term financial goals.
- (D) Explain opportunity cost in spending and saving decisions.
- (E) Understand emergency savings and financial security planning.

#### (6) Saving and Investing
- (A) Explain the importance of saving early and compound interest.
- (B) Compare saving and investment instruments (savings accounts, CDs, stocks, bonds, mutual funds, retirement plans).
- (C) Assess risk vs. return and diversification.
- (D) Examine inflation's impact on purchasing power.

#### (7) Credit and Debt Management
- (A) Explain how credit works and the role of credit reports and scores.
- (B) Compare types of credit (installment, revolving, secured, unsecured).
- (C) Evaluate advantages and disadvantages of using credit.
- (D) Identify signs of excessive debt and strategies for management.
- (E) Explain predatory lending practices and borrower protections.

#### (8) Consumer Skills and Protection
- (A) Analyze consumer rights and responsibilities.
- (B) Identify consumer protection agencies and laws.
- (C) Evaluate marketing and advertising strategies influencing decisions.
- (D) Understand contracts, warranties, and return policies.

#### (9) Insurance and Risk Management
- (A) Identify types of insurance (health, auto, home, life, disability).
- (B) Evaluate cost-benefit of insurance coverage.
- (C) Explain risk management strategies for financial protection.

#### (10) Retirement and Estate Planning
- (A) Describe retirement plans (401(k), IRA, pension).
- (B) Explain tax advantages of retirement savings.
- (C) Discuss estate planning tools (wills, trusts, power of attorney).
